Hi, I'm Dhilan.

My parents immigrated to the U.S. from Cape Town and London. I was born in New York and raised in Los Angeles.

I've always liked figuring out how things work. I built my first PC when I was nine, and by eleven I was building them for friends. That curiosity has guided everything I've done since.

At Loyola High School, I served as Class President and helped manage a $1.3 million scholarship portfolio as part of the Student Investment Fund. These experiences gave me an early glance at what real responsibility feels like—when decisions have numbers, names, and real outcomes attached.

As an upperclassman, I wanted to create something that would last beyond me. I wanted to sustain a program for generations to come. That's how my Composting Program started.

Two years later, we've composted over 10,000 lbs of waste and brought in more than 200 students. It's now the first fully student-led, on-site high school composting program in the country.

Aristotle once said, "We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit." Sustainability works the same way—not as a trend, but as a daily habit that adds up for the future generations' reward.

I like music, cooking, and golf—a sport that is deceptively simple and endlessly complicated, reflecting life in every swing.
